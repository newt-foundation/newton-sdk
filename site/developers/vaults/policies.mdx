Newton vault policies combine Rego policy logic with policy data oracles. The oracle gathers the external data needed for a decision, then the Rego policy evaluates that data against the vault's configured parameters.

In the Newton dashboard, use **Explore Data Oracles** to browse available data oracles, inspect the data they provide, and add the relevant oracle to a policy. Vault policies can combine multiple oracles, so a single vault action can be checked against risk, compliance, and market data before it is approved.

## Available Policy Types

### Vaults.fyi Vault Risk Rating

The Vaults.fyi policy checks whether a vault still fits the risk profile configured by the vault owner or curator.

It can deny an action when:

- APY spikes beyond the configured z-score threshold
- TVL drawdown exceeds the configured 24 hour or 7 day limit
- Vault risk score falls below the configured floor
- The vault allocation has changed since the last known allocation hash
- Vaults.fyi reports a high or critical flag
- The vault is marked as corrupted

Use this policy when you want vault actions to be gated by live vault health, not just static onchain configuration.

### Chainalysis Address Screening

The Chainalysis policy screens an address for sanctions and risk categories.

It can deny an action when:

- The address appears on sanctions data
- The address is categorized as high risk
- The address has a risk category included in the vault's blocklist

Use this policy for vault deposits, withdrawals, curator operations, or other flows where the vault needs address-level compliance and AML controls.

### Webacy Depositor Reputation

The Webacy policy evaluates depositor wallet reputation before allowing a deposit or other user action.

It can deny an action when:

- The address is sanctioned
- The address is high risk
- The address has too much exposure to exploit, hack, drainer, or stolen-fund signals
- A medium-risk depositor attempts to deposit more than the configured USD cap

Use this policy when a vault wants a risk-sensitive intake flow. Lower-risk users can proceed, while higher-risk or larger deposits can be restricted based on the vault's rules.

### RedStone Oracle Divergence

The RedStone policy compares a RedStone price feed against an onchain oracle and checks whether the divergence is acceptable.

It can deny an action when:

- The RedStone feed is stale
- Price divergence is above a hard deny threshold
- Divergence stays above a warning threshold for a sustained period

Use this policy when vault operations depend on market data freshness or when reallocation, cap changes, or other actions should stop if oracle data appears unreliable.

## Choosing Policies

| Goal | Policy type |
| --- | --- |
| Check vault health and risk profile | Vaults.fyi Vault Risk Rating |
| Screen addresses for sanctions or risk categories | Chainalysis Address Screening |
| Gate deposits based on wallet reputation | Webacy Depositor Reputation |
| Stop actions during stale or divergent oracle conditions | RedStone Oracle Divergence |

## Composing Policies

Vault policies are most useful when combined. For example, a regulated yield vault might require:

- A Webacy depositor reputation check before deposit
- Chainalysis sanctions screening for the depositor address
- Vaults.fyi risk checks before accepting additional capital
- RedStone oracle divergence checks before curator reallocations

The result is a vault whose rules are explicit, inspectable, and enforced before execution.
